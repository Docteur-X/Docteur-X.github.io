Quadratic Equations \( \textcolor{blue}{a} x^2 + \textcolor{orange}{b} x + \textcolor{green}{c} \) have solutions \( x = \frac{ - \textcolor{orange}{b} \pm \sqrt{\textcolor{orange}{b}^2-4 \textcolor{blue}{a} \textcolor{green}{c}} }{ 2 \textcolor{blue}{a}} \)

Setting up the question here. \( \textcolor{blue}{a} , \textcolor{orange}{b} , \textcolor{green}{c} \) are real numbers that you know, while \( x \) is a number that we don't know but satisfies the equation you'll see in a bit.

If you have an equation that looks like this: \[ \textcolor{blue}{a} x^2 + \textcolor{orange}{b} x + \textcolor{green}{c} \] and you want to find number(s) for \( x \) that satisfies the equation, the answer is:

\[ x = \frac{ - \textcolor{orange}{b} \pm \sqrt{\textcolor{orange}{b}^2-4 \textcolor{blue}{a} \textcolor{green}{c}} }{ 2 \textcolor{blue}{a}} \]

You sometimes get 2 answers, you sometimes get 0 answers, and you might sometimes get only 1 answer for \( x \). These tricky possibilities is handled automatically by the equation for you.
