I'm going to introduce a very specific type of vector here. Vectors are an algebraic thing; they are often denoted like this on paper \( \vec{v} \), and in boldface in text \( \bm{v} \). You can add vectors together \( \vec{v} + \vec{w} \) and scale vectors by a number \( a\vec{v} \). The vectors we'll see below are also essentially an arrow in 2D or 3D space; like arrows, they have a well-defined length/size and points in a fixed direction. This allows us to represent geometric lines algebraically. This is super helpful because we can understand things like positions and movements of things in 3D space not only geometrically but algebraically too.
