Note before we begin.

\(i = \sqrt{-1}\). Actually, \( \sqrt{-1} = \pm i \) is more correct. Square roots and cube roots and other roots are complicated, so forget about roots for now. I'll show you how to understand complex numbers more clearly.

What are Complex Numbers?

We pretend that there is a number called \( i \) so that \( i^2 = i \cdot i = -1 \).

No real number has such a property, because if \( x \) is a real number, \( x \cdot x \geq 0 \). For example, if \( i = -1 \), then \( i^2 = -1 \cdot -1 = 1 \), which is just wrong, so \( i \neq -1 \). If \( i=-2 \), \( i^2 = -2 \cdot -2 = 4 \) is also wrong. So \( i \neq -1 \), \( i\ \neq -2 \), and hopefully you can see that \( i \neq 2, i \neq 0, i \neq -4.5, i \neq \pi , \) and \( i \neq \) any real number.

If \(i\) couldn't be any real number, you may insist that it doesn't exist. Let's pretend it does exist as a different kind of 'imaginary' number. Somehow by doing this, we don't break any old maths, but we get a new, self-consistent number system of real numbers and imaginary numbers and a mix of them called complex numbers.

There are 4 things you can do with numbers: you can add 2 numbers together, you can subtract one number from another, you can multiply numbers together, and you can divide a number by another number that isn't 0. \( i \neq 0 \) so we can divide numbers by \(i\).

The rules are exactly the same as the real numbers you're familiar with. Look through the algebra rules in "algebra-summary.html", and the rules for complex numbers are exactly the same.

Any complex number can be written like this: (real number) + (real number)\( \cdot i\). The only extra rule in complex numbers is that \( i^2 = i \cdot i = -1 \).

If you want the rules to be written out explicitly, here they are:

\[ (a+ib) + (c+id) = a + i \cdot b + c + i \cdot d = a+c + i \cdot b + i \cdot d = (a + c) + i \cdot (b+d) \]

\[ (a+ib) - (c+id) = (a+i \cdot b) + (-1) \cdot (c + i \cdot d) = ( a+ib ) + ( - c - i d ) = a + i b - c - i d = a - c + i b - i d = (a-c) + i (b-d) \]

\[ (a+ib)(c+id) = a \cdot (c+id) + i\cdot b \cdot (c+id) = a \cdot c + a \cdot i d + i \cdot b \cdot c + ib \cdot id = ac + iad + i bc + \cancel{i^2}{-1} \cdot bd = ac - bd + i(ad + bc) \]
