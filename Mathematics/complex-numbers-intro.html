What are Complex Numbers?

You may have heard of \(i = \sqrt{-1}\). Later you'll see that one should actually write \( \sqrt{-1} = \pm i \) instead. We'll see that taking square roots and cube roots tend to create multiple values, while most other ordinary operations are much nicer, inputing just one value outputs one unique value, so forget about square roots for now. I'll show you how to understand complex numbers in a clearer way.

Any complex number can be written like this: (real number)+(real number)\( \cdot i\). There are 4 things you can do with complex numbers: you can add 2 complex numbers together, you can subtract one complex number from another, you can multiply complex numbers together, and you can divide a (possibly zero) complex number by another (nonzero) number. The rules are exactly the same as the real numbers you're familiar with.
